# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Community-minded Cursor users who browse the board to see who's active in their country or globally. They arrive to discover peers, compare public profile stats, and share their standing with others. Context is casual and social: a quick check between sessions, a link shared in Slack or X, or a country-filtered browse to find local power users.

## Product Purpose

Cursor Leaderboard ranks public Cursor profile stats (agents, tokens, streaks) so the community can see who is most active and celebrate that activity together. Success means people join, find their rank, and share it: viral loops through posts, invites, and country-specific boards that give everyone a reason to participate.

## Brand Personality

Native Cursor. The interface should feel like a natural extension of cursor.com: same chrome, same orange accent, same restrained dark tooling aesthetic. Voice is direct and confident, not hype-driven. Competition is present but understated; the board reads as community discovery, not a game.

Three words: **native**, **social**, **restrained**.

## Anti-references

- Crypto and gaming leaderboard aesthetics: neon glows, dark purple gradients, over-the-top rank badges, particle effects, "LEVEL UP" energy
- Generic SaaS dashboard slop: hero metric blocks, identical card grids, gradient text, glassmorphism chrome
- Aggressive gamification: XP bars, loot-box metaphors, streak flames, shouty CTAs

## Design Principles

1. **Feel like Cursor, not a fan site.** Reuse cursor.com tokens, spacing, and tone so the board feels official-adjacent, not third-party novelty.
2. **Design for the share moment.** Every join flow and rank display should produce something worth posting: clear rank, country context, profile link.
3. **Community over individual vanity.** Country filters, ambassador badges, and browsable lists prioritize discovering peers, not just flexing a number.
4. **Restrained competition.** Ranks and metrics are prominent but never scream; no casino energy, no neon, no gamification chrome.
5. **Public data, honest presentation.** Stats come from public profiles; show them clearly without embellishment or misleading emphasis.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard-navigable filters and join flow, sufficient contrast on dark chrome, readable tabular numbers. Rank indicators should not rely on color alone (use numerals and position). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any transitions.
